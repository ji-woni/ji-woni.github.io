Pericytes Drive Pulmonary Vascular Remodeling via HIF2α Signaling

Identifying a novel cellular driver of hypoxia-induced vascular disease in the lung

Pulmonary Hypertension Pericytes HIF2α Vascular Remodeling Hypoxia Mouse Models PCLS

Overview

Pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) is characterized by progressive, obliterative remodeling of the small pulmonary arteries — a process classically attributed to dysfunction in endothelial cells and smooth muscle cells. This work challenges that canonical view by demonstrating that pericytes, a perivascular cell population that wraps around small vessels, are active contributors to vascular remodeling in a HIF2α-dependent manner.

Using conditional knockout mouse models and precision-cut lung slices, we show that pericyte-specific loss of HIF2α is protective against hypoxia-induced vascular remodeling — placing pericytes upstream of the structural changes that define PAH and suggesting new therapeutic entry points.

Background & Motivation

Hypoxia is a potent trigger for pulmonary vascular remodeling. At altitude or during chronic lung disease, reduced oxygen activates the hypoxia-inducible factor (HIF) transcriptional program. HIF2α, in particular, is expressed in vascular endothelial cells and has been linked to PAH — but its role in non-endothelial perivascular cells had not been systematically investigated.

Pericytes are poorly understood in the context of pulmonary vascular disease. They stabilize vessel walls, regulate blood flow, and communicate bidirectionally with endothelial cells. Our hypothesis was that pericytes under hypoxic stress activate HIF2α-driven programs that destabilize the vascular wall and prime pathological remodeling.

Experimental Approach

Pericyte-Specific Conditional Knockouts

We generated mice with pericyte-specific deletion of HIF2α (using a PDGFRβ-Cre driver), allowing us to isolate the pericyte-autonomous contribution to remodeling in the context of intact endothelial and smooth muscle cell HIF2α.

Hypoxia-Induced PAH Model

Animals were housed in a normobaric hypoxia chamber (10% O₂) for three weeks — a well-validated model that recapitulates the hemodynamic and structural hallmarks of PAH: elevated RVSP, right ventricular hypertrophy, and medial wall thickening.

Precision Cut Lung Slices (PCLS)

PCLS were prepared from control and conditional knockout mice to directly assess vascular contractility and pericyte morphology in an ex vivo preparation that preserves vessel architecture and cell–cell contacts.

Primary Pericyte Culture

Pulmonary pericytes were isolated and cultured under normoxic and hypoxic conditions to map HIF2α target gene expression and assess paracrine effects on co-cultured endothelial cells.

Key Findings

  • HIF2α was a key mediator in driving pericytes to participate in distal vascular remodeling and muscularization.
  • Pericyte-selective HIF2α overexpression in mice exacerbates pulmonary hypertension and right ventricular hypertrophy.
